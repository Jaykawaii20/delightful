What's happening about Tourism, Culture and Events in the Philippines

The Philippines' tourism industry achieved an all-time high in international visitor revenues in 2024, with earnings reaching approximately PHP 760 billion. This figure surpasses pre-pandemic levels by 19%, indicating a robust recovery and growth in the sector.

Record-Breaking Tourism Revenue in 2024

Cebu has garnered international acclaim by being ranked third in the "Awards 2024," highlighting its appeal as a premier tourist destination. This recognition underscores Cebu's rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and its significant role in bolstering the country's tourism industry.

Recognition of Cebu as a Top Destination

Siargao, often hailed as the "surfing capital of the Philippines," has experienced a remarkable resurgence in tourism. In 2023, the island welcomed 529,822 visitors, a significant increase from the 125,088 tourists recorded in 2022. This surge includes 476,074 domestic and 53,748 international travelers, with foreign arrivals soaring by 511.89% compared to the previous year.
